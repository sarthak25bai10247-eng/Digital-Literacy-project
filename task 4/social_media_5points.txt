=====================================================================
   RESPONSIBLE SOCIAL MEDIA USE — A COLLEGE STUDENT'S GUIDE
   5 Big Points to Keep in Mind
=====================================================================


1. THINK BEFORE YOU POST
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Always ask yourself if you'd be okay with your professor, parent,
or future employer seeing what you're about to post. The internet
never forgets, and what feels funny or harmless today can seriously
hurt your reputation tomorrow. If you're feeling emotional or angry,
step away from your phone and come back later — posts made in the
heat of the moment almost always end up being regretted.


2. PROTECT YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Keep your home address, daily routine, financial details, and
real-time location off public posts. Oversharing online is one of
the easiest ways to put yourself at risk. Check your privacy
settings regularly so you always know who can actually see your
content, and trust your gut when a follow request or message feels
off — it's okay to decline or block.


3. BE KIND AND RESPONSIBLE ONLINE
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Treat people online the same way you would treat them in person —
with respect and decency. Leave supportive comments, celebrate your
friends, and stay far away from public drama or hate. Remember that
rude or hurtful comments say more about you than the person you're
targeting, and online behaviour has very real consequences in
real life.


4. STOP AND FACT-CHECK BEFORE SHARING
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Not everything that looks viral is true. Before you hit share on a
news story, health tip, or shocking claim, take 10 seconds to
Google it. Spreading false information — even accidentally — can
mislead people and damage your own credibility. If you are not sure
whether something is accurate, the safest move is simply not to
share it.


5. PROTECT YOUR TIME AND MENTAL HEALTH
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Social media is designed to keep you scrolling, and it is very easy
to lose hours without realising it. Set a daily screen time limit
that actually works for you, take regular breaks, and pay attention
to how certain apps or accounts make you feel. If something
consistently makes you feel bad about yourself, unfollow it without
guilt. Your focus, sleep, and mental health matter far more than
any streak or follower count.


=====================================================================
   Stay smart. Stay kind. Log off when you need to.
   Real life is worth showing up for too.
=====================================================================
